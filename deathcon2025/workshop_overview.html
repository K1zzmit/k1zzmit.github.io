This workshop follows a progressive learning approach: start with foundations, build understanding, then apply advanced concepts. The first half of this workshop is instructional and it is recommended to watch the video before conducting the hands-on exercises.
1
What is Threat Hunting?
  • Proactive search for hidden threats in your environment.
  • Threat hunting is more than responding to alerts; hunters ask questions and investigate.
  • Three main approaches: anomaly-based, intelligence-driven, hypothesis-driven.
2
What is a Log?
  • Logs are the breadcrumbs of digital activity.
  • Key types: Windows Event Logs, Sysmon, network, authentication.
  • Quality and coverage of logs are more important than quantity.
3
What is a SIEM?
  • SIEMs collect, correlate, and alert on security data.
  • Popular options: Splunk, ELK, Sentinel, Chronicle.
  • Effective hunting depends on good queries and data.
4
Threat Hunting in Splunk
  • Splunk SPL allows you to search and analyze security data.
  • Start simple, refine queries, and look for patterns.
  • Document findings and share with your team.
